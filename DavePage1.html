Background Summary

As I stated on the site entry page, I have acquired an extremely broad and varied set of knowledge, skills and experience in my careers and avocations up to now. I have shown myself to be flexible and extremely adaptable. I have always been a self-starter and I am used to working without need for close supervision. However, I have solid experience working in close collaborative environments as well.

I worked for three years as a head office Life and Casualty insurance underwriter and for two years as a lipid biochemist in my younger years. I practiced clinical medicine for over 26 years in varied practice settings. Over time, I have come to focus my interests on solving quantitative questions about the data collected. I have already completed an MBA with a Graduate Certificate in Health Administration in 2003.

I have pursued many interests in operations research and analytics at over the years with formal courses, many projects and extensive self-directed study and research. I refer to my resume outlining many of these experiences. I am preparing for certification as Java Programmer 1 and for Base SAS certification also.

I am experienced with many technologies including Microsoft Project, PowerPoint, Excel, Word, Access and Visio. I am particularly familiar with Excel use and the R statistical language. I have studied process simulation with Arena® and programming of models (in Matlab, FORTRAN and Java). I have worked with database systems for years and recently have taken more formal courses in Database Management and SQL. I am comfortable writing HTML/CSS/JavaScript particularly as web interfaces to business applications and databases.

My experience with SAS includes a full range of Data Step functions and manipulations as well as procedure step functions of SAS/Stat. I have written SAS macros, SQL queries with SAS/SQL and matrix manipulation programs using PROC IML. Though I have been programming rather informally with Fortran and Basic for years, I have taken courses in Object Oriented Programming with Java and online courses in Data Structures and Algorithms with Java and the Analysis of Algorithms via Coursera Foundation (Prof. Sedgwick, Princeton University).

I am interested in healthcare information systems and have used various Electronic Medical Record Systems as part of my clinical work. I Write technical articles, most often but far from exclusively on biomedical topics. Samples of my writing are accessible to interested readers on this website. Follow my copy writing links or have a look at my Blog pages. I am quite experienced with development of presentations with PowerPoint as well as the use of all Microsoft Office applications. I am in particular very familiar with Excel use and the R statistical language. My usual programming tool is Java. I have used Visual Basic rather extensively in the past and have an associated familiarity with VBA.
